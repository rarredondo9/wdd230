Visual Hierarchy

  • BYU-Pathway
  • byupathway.edu

    • On the BYU Pathway home page, Visual Hierarchy is exemplified by showing first the most critical action—applying—using a large, colorful button that stands out against the rest of the page. Key information such as program benefits and requirements are laid out in a clear, descending order of importance, guiding the viewer's eye from the main heading to supporting details, and finally to the call to action.

    White Space and Clean Design

  • Apple
  • apple.com

    • Apple’s homepage is a prime example of white space and clean design. The layout features a lot of open space around text and images, creating a clean and uncluttered look. This not only makes the content more legible but also allows the products and call-to-action buttons to stand out, making navigation intuitive for users.

    PARC: Repetition

  • Canva
  • canva.com

    • On Canva's homepage, the principle of repetition is likely showcased through the consistent use of brand colors, uniform typography, and repeated design motifs across various sections. This repetition helps to unify the different parts of the site, making it easier for users to navigate and recognize Canva’s brand identity.
